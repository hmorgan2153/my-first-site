Enterprise Data Architecture

Enterprise Data Architecture is defined as:

"The processes and practices that leverages a master set of Conceptual, Logical and Physical data models to support the management of common corporate data, metadata and taxonomies for the evolving needs of the business and to provide a business stategy that guides the integration, usage, quality improvement anf effective delivery of data."

The dilemma facing an organization that wants to achieve a well-managed Enterprise Data Architecture program is determing if this a Requirement or a Roadblock? The questions that are frequently asked are:

  • What level of investment is required?
  • How is progress measured?
  • When will the corporation see a benefit?
  • How do we get started?
